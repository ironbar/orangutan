Animal-AI Olympics rules

PLEASE READ THESE rules CAREFULLY. Entry into the Animal-AI Olympics constitutes acceptance of these rules. If you do not agree to any part of these rules, do not enter the competition. Terms used Competition: The Animal-AI Olympics as described here and on the competition website. Competition website: Refers to the main website for the competition http://animalaiolympics.com/ and either of the three sites linked from the main website, https://github.com/beyretb/AnimalAI-Olympics, https://mdcrosby.com/blog and https://evalai.cloudcv.org/ (Animal-AI-specific link forthcoming). Competition organisers: The organisers of the Animal-AI Olympics competition as listed on the competition website, including, where applicable, affiliated institutions. Prize Provider: Any provider of any prize, or portion thereof, for the competition. Dates. The competition commences at 11:59pm GMT on July 1st. Uploading on the competition website will be available by 11:59pm GMT on July 8th. The first test phase will be at 11:59pm AOE on September 1st. Submissions past this date will not be counted. If there is enough funding to run extended testing the test phase will contain additional small variations of the tests and participants score may therefore differ from that received during the main competition phase. Results from this phase will be used for the distribution of AWS credits prizes. Prizes will be distributed in a commercially reasonable time-frame. Depending on progress, additional, more complex tests, may be added to the test set once results have been collated. The second and final test phase will be at 11:59pm AOE on November 1st. Submissions past this date will not be counted. If there is enough funding to run extended testing the test phase will contain additional small variations of the tests and participants score may therefore differ from that received during the main competition phase. Results will be announced by November 15th. Prizes will be distributed in a commercially reasonable time-frame. Note that Competition deadlines are subject to change should they need to for any reason. Any additional or altered deadlines will be publicized via the Competition Website. Honour Code In addition to the behaviours outlined by the official competition rules, "cheating" encompasses any attempt to gain an edge by attempting to access information in a way that is not intended. Examples of cheating include (but are not limited to): Attempting to abuse the competition infrastructure to gain an edge; Attempting to obtain information about the configuration files used for the tests beyond the information available on competition website; Attempting to use any information from the environment during testing beyond the input given to the agent. Please note that we reserve the right, but do not have the obligation, to review submitted code to disqualify any contributions demonstrating cheating, and we reserve the right to determine what competition organisers believe, in their sole discretion, constitutes cheating. We respectfully request that you refrain from cheating. Entry You may enter the competition as an individual or as a member of a Team. Individuals eligible to participate in the competition (see below) may form a "Team" which must include at least one, but not more than a maximum of five individuals. An individual may only be a member of one Team. Teams will appoint one member to be the lead for their Team ("Team Lead"). Team Lead will be responsible for submitting the Team's entry and will be the point of contact of all official communications between the Team and competition organisers and will receive all prizes on behalf of the Team. Team Leads are responsible for obtaining all consents from their Team members and ensuring that each member of the Team agrees to these rules. Entering the competition constitutes acceptance of these rules for every member of the team. Each Team may submit an entry as often as they like, but only the first entry each day will be submitted for testing. Competition organisers are not responsible for an entry or part of an entry that they do not receive for any reason, or for entries that they receive but are inaccessible or illegible for any reason and such entries may be disqualified at Competition Organiser's sole discretion.
